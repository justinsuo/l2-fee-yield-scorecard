What this tells us

The market still prices L2s on TVL and narrative. This scorecard prices them on the metric that actually accrues value to a token: fee yield — annualized network fees divided by the capital sitting on the chain. It's the on-chain analogue of revenue-on-assets.

The ZK angle. The general-purpose ZK rollups (Scroll, Starknet, Taiko, zkSync) show the structural tension worth underwriting: proving is still a real cost line, and until prover costs compress and liquidity stops fragmenting across a dozen chains, fee revenue stays thin relative to the capital they've attracted. The investable question isn't "is ZK the endgame" (it is) — it's which teams reach durable fee yield before incentives run out. I'd want to see fee yield trending up independent of token emissions before underwriting at primary-round prices.

Method: TVL from /v2/chains; fees from /overview/fees/<chain> (30d, annualized ×12.17); yield = annualized fees ÷ TVL. Fees are a proxy for protocol revenue, not token cash flow — directionally right, not GAAP. Chains with sub-$10M measured TVL, or an implied yield over 100% (a tell that DefiLlama is undercounting that chain's TVL), are marked n/m and kept out of the ranking rather than flattering it.
